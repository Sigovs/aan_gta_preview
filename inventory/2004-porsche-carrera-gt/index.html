Overview

Porsche built a Le Mans engine, could not race it, and put it in a road car instead — 5.7 litres, ten cylinders, 8,400 rpm, and a ceramic clutch the size of a dinner plate. No turbos, no paddles, no driver aids worth the name: six speeds, three pedals, and a beechwood knob. Six hundred and forty-four came to America. This is one of them, in GT Silver over dark grey, under five thousand miles.
